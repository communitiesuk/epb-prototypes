What does the rating mean?
Properties get a score from 100 (best) to 1 (worst). A better score means the property needs less energy to stay at a comfortable temperature.

The score is based on how much energy a property typically needs to be heated above 18°C in winter and cooled below 24°C in summer.

Each score also has a letter rating from A (best) to G (worst):
  • A rating - 90 to 100
  • B rating - 80 to 89
  • C rating - 70 to 79
  • D rating - 60 to 69
  • E rating - 50 to 59
  • F rating - 40 to 49
  • G rating - 1 to 39
