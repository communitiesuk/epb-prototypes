Air source heat pump

This involves installing an outdoor unit that takes heat from the outside air, and an indoor unit that transfers the heat to your central heating and hot water system.

Benefits

Savings

£100 - £300 per year. It will take 24 - 150 years to pay back the initial investment.

Market appeal

Heat pumps are occasionally found in England. Installing one can make the property stand out.

Environmental impact

Reduces carbon emissions by a small amount (0.2 tonnes per year).

How are these calculated?

Savings: This is based on estimated heating and hot water cost savings for a two-person household. It is updated every year to reflect changes in energy prices.

Market appeal: This is estimated using national housing survey data. It is updated every year.

Environmental impact: This is based on estimated energy savings for a two-person household.

Cost

This is calculated based on average costs for similar properties in the South West.
Cost of materials £4,000 - £8,000
Cost of labour £3,000 - £7,000
Total cost £7,000 - £15,000

What to expect

Disruption

Expect a high level of disruption. Installation usually takes 2 to 5 days. The work can cause noise and loss of heating and hot water for the duration of the installation. Occupants may have access to some living spaces disrupted while work is being completed.

Lifespan

Air source heat pumps are built to last around 15 - 20 years. A professional servicing is recommended every year.
