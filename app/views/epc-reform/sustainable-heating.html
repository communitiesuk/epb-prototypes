What does the rating mean?
Properties get a score from 100 (best) to 1 (worst). A better score means the property is more energy efficient and produces fewer carbon emissions.   The score is based on the energy efficiency and carbon emissions of a property’s heating, hot water and cooking.
  • A rating - 90 to 100
  • B rating - 80 to 89
  • C rating - 70 to 79
  • D rating - 60 to 69
  • E rating - 50 to 59
  • F rating - 40 to 49
  • G rating - 1 to 39

How to improve the rating and score

These steps show how to improve this property’s rating and score. They are ordered to give the biggest score increase for the lowest cost. The costs are based on regional averages and are updated every year.

Get expert advice before you start any building work. The score increase for each step is an estimate based and is not guaranteed.

Step 1: Solar water heating

What does this involve?
This involves installing panels on the roof which collect heat from the sun, and installing a pump and a hot water storage tank inside the property.
  • Costs £3,000 - £6,000
  • 15 - 20 year lifespan
  • Medium disruption

Step 2: Air source heat pump

What does this involve?
This involves installing an outdoor unit that takes heat from the outside air, and an indoor unit that transfers the heat to your central heating and hot water system.
  • Costs £7,000 - £15,000
  • 15 - 20 year lifespan
  • High disruption
