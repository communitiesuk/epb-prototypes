What is the Energy efficiency rating?

Properties get an Energy efficiency score from 100 (best) to 1 (worst). A better score means the property costs less to run.

The score is based on how much it typically costs to provide a property’s heating, hot water and lighting.

Each score also has a letter rating from A (best) to G (worst):

  • A rating - 90 to 100
  • B rating - 80 to 89
  • C rating - 70 to 79
  • D rating - 60 to 69
  • E rating - 50 to 59
  • F rating - 40 to 49
  • G rating - 1 to 39

How to improve the Energy efficiency rating

These steps show how to improve this property’s rating and score. The steps are ordered to give the biggest score increase for the lowest cost. The costs are based on regional averages and are updated every year.

Get expert advice before you start any building work. The score increase for each step is an estimate and is not guaranteed.

Step 1: Roof insulation

What does this involve?
This involves adding insulation under the roof structure or above the ceiling.
  • Costs £700 - £1,200
  • Low disruption
  • 40 - 100 year lifespan

What does this involve?
This involves installing solar panels on the roof, and an inverter which connects to the property’s electrical system.
