Use this page to find data on the:

  • number of certificates uploaded to the Energy Performance of Buildings Register

How to use this data

All certificates uploaded to the Energy Performance of Buildings Register are shown. Some certificates may have expired or been replaced.

Energy certificates are not:

  • required for every property in the UK
  • directly comparable over time, due to changes in methodology
  • a direct indicator of housing supply or the state of the housing or rental market

This data is not classed as official statistics. Find out what this means.

This data is updated monthly and covers:

  • England
  • Wales
  • Northern Ireland

For data in Scotland, visit the Scottish Energy Performance Certificate Register.

Can't find what you are looking for?

For more detailed energy certificate data, check the data published on Open Data Communities. This data is updated monthly.

What does this mean?
Energy performance for new properties and new conversions is calculated using the SAP methodology. It is calculated by an assessor with access to the building specifications.

Energy performance for existing properties is calculated using the RdSAP methodology. It is calculated by assuming certain building specifications based on the year that the property was constructed.

Energy efficiency rating

What does this mean?
Energy efficiency rating (EER) is a metric based on how much it costs to heat and light a home to an agreed standard. It is calculated as a numerical score that typically ranges between 1 (high cost) and 100 (low cost).

The numerical score can be presented as a letter range between A and G as shown below:

A rating – 92 plus

B rating – 81 to 91

C rating – 69 to 80

D rating – 55 to 68

E rating – 39 to 54

F rating – 21 to 38

G rating – 1 to 20
